Interests

Besides being a full-time student and coding, I enjoy skateboarding, reading Webtoons, atching TV shows, and spending time with my friends. I have been skating for a few months now and I am still trying to learn how to ollie. Currently, my favorite two Webtoons are Lookism and Viral Hit, both written by the same author! I am currently watching Better Call Saul and it is fantastic. During the school year, I usually spend time with my friends by studying or doing homework with their presence and sometimes, grab food.

Ultimately, I love helping other people out. Specifically, I like to share my knowledge, if I can. My two biggest strengths lie in programming and mathematics. For the most part, math is challenging and every teacher wants things done their way, so helping others in math is tricky. On the other hand, coding is very consistent. Seeing people's faces light up when their code compiles and executes correctly with a little guidance gives me a giant dopamine boost and sense of purpose.

Nowadays, young people learn to code through their school in some curriculum. They learn key concepts and apply them with projects that their teacher creates and assigns to them. However, not everyone has the luxury of growing up in a school with computers, or have stable Internet access at home. Or maybe they haven't picked up an interest in coding until later in life - when they are out of school and going to college just isn't viable.

So I am currently working on a life-long project where a devise and assign projects in a progessive manner. People will be able to self-teach themselves and learn the fundamentals of programming without the need to spend a significant amount of money for college. How they learn the material is still fuzzy, but in the grand scheme of the project, they will be able to learn everything for free!
